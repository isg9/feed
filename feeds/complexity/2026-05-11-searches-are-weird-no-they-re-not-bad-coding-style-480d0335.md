# Searches Are Weird! No they're not! Bad coding style?

In David Marcus's guest post on good coding style (see [here](https://blog.computationalcomplexity.org/2025/09/guest-post-on-why-coding-style-is.html))  he reviewed a book from 1986 called  "Professional Pascal."

I wondered if it was still in print and could be bought:

1) I went to Amazon and searched all products for Professional Pascal. I got [this](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Professional+Pascal&crid=3M44XLK8NZCHV&sprefix=professional+pascal%2Caps%2C119&ref=nb_sb_noss), which is not that book.

2) I then restricted to books, and I got the same, though later on the page I got a relevant book, [here](https://www.amazon.com/Software-Tools-Pascal-Brian-Kernighan/dp/0201103427/ref=sr_1_12?crid=2VX91OG6W7B8E&dib=eyJ2IjoiMSJ9.mBMsTXgBzD3DXBWcO03buyu0VYwqLwycV0sNcI2qZIKDMBWW-1OfyNv0odjgqpC0430Cf6vymFQg6wdFiB-gvobFoBrV0Es_50Kbfvzx54QFxg8SfnSJkPQ5TTQN6vdY7eVzlQUJXvZ6X82FU9rc38Rj3Wb0C_VKdYDbgqM--SxErZNqlzTrsgzyTixJ-sMUKMotodPAyZ0RZTiSR6UMtfRvOFgjr8N4i_W99fbchIw.z85URUshKcn_aFlzbNL1pi9VJ9zGE6XDxVv5SoXLTYg&dib_tag=se&keywords=Professional+Pascal&qid=1755976796&s=books&sprefix=professional+pascal%2Cstripbooks%2C150&sr=1-12).

3) I then searched for Professional Pascal on Google, and got the Amazon site for the book [here](https://www.amazon.com/Professional-Pascal-Essays-Practice-Programming/dp/0201117762).

David thought this was weird. I did not. As I put it:

*A computer does something which makes no sense. This is common, hence it's not weird.*

Why did the search from *outside* of Amazon do better than the search *inside* of Amazon?

Speculation

1) Search is just a really hard problem.

2) The coders at Amazon did not use good coding style. They should read the book. If they can find it.
