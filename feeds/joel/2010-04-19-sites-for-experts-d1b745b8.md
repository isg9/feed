# Sites for experts

Since announcing [the new plans for Stack Exchange](http://blog.stackexchange.com/post/518474918/stack-exchange-2-0), there’s been a lot of discussion about what kind of new Q&A sites will work best on this platform.

S[![](https://i0.wp.com/www.joelonsoftware.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/04/19stackex-thumbnail.png?w=730&ssl=1)](https://i0.wp.com/www.joelonsoftware.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/04/19stackex.png?ssl=1)o far there are [32 informal proposals](http://meta.stackexchange.com/questions/tagged?tagnames=site-proposal&sort=votes&pagesize=15) on [meta.stackexchange.com](http://meta.stackexchange.com/). We’re weeks away from opening a site where these proposals can become real.

From [my answer](http://meta.stackexchange.com/questions/5528/proposal-for-a-firearms-se/5530#5530) to the Firearms proposal: “The power of the Stack Exchange platform is detailed, *expert* answers to extremely rare, ‘long-tail,’ highly technical questions. To get expert answers, you need experts. To attract experts, you need a site where people are asking very interesting and hard questions, not the basic questions, so that it’s clear that this is a PRO site, not a consumer/enthusiast site…. and remember, the pro sites WILL attract the enthusiasts, but not the other way around.”

From [my answer](http://meta.stackexchange.com/questions/5533/proposal-for-a-law-se/5546#5546) to the Law proposal: “There are only 200 easy law questions, and they’ve all been asked 100 times on Mahalo and Yahoo!Answers. But there are 20,000,000 detailed, difficult, long-tail questions that only professionals can answer, and we’d be doing a REAL service to the Internet by creating a place where you can find answers to the 20,000,000 hard questions, not the 200 easy ones.”

Our core mission at Stack Overflow is:

*Make the Internet a better place to get expert answers to your questions.*

The “expert” part of that mission is important, otherwise we’re just building another place for the same questions that every other Q&A site has. We want the law Stack Exchange to attract lawyers, the movie Stack Exchange to attract filmmakers, and the aviation Stack Exchange to attract pilots. What makes a community great is great people… that is, real experts. And the experts want to hang out with other experts.
