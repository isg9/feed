# Founders at Work

[Jessica Livingston](http://www.foundersatwork.com/jessicalivingston.html), a partner at [Y Combinator](http://ycombinator.com/), has just published a book of interviews with 32 [high tech company founders](http://www.foundersatwork.com/interviews.html) called [Founders at Work](http://www.foundersatwork.com/index.html).

[![](https://i0.wp.com/www.joelonsoftware.com/wp-content/uploads/2007/01/30FoundersAtWork.png?w=730&ssl=1)](http://www.foundersatwork.com/) She posted a couple of chapters online, including the [interview with me](http://www.foundersatwork.com/joelspolsky.html), which is probably the most complete story of the early days of Fog Creek Software in print.

“There’s a bunch of people out there doing certain types of things and they seem to be pretty incompetent, but they’re getting huge valuations. Surely if I did those same things, knowing that I am less incompetent—merely semi-incompetent as opposed to extremely incompetent—I should be able to achieve at least their level of success.”

I haven’t read the book yet… still waiting for my copy to arrive from Amazon, which appears to be beyond semi-incompetent with regards to what “overnight shipping” might mean when you order something on Thursday. Check out the [list of interviews](http://www.foundersatwork.com/interviews.html), though: virtually everyone starting a high tech company will be all over this book like senators on cake.
