# Outsmarting your airline

Last Friday, I was sitting at the San Diego airport, trying to figure out if my flight was going to be on time.

Of course, Continental thinks it will:

![](https://i0.wp.com/www.joelonsoftware.com/wp-content/uploads/2007/10/13-01.jpg?w=730&ssl=1)

Being suspicious, I notice that there’s no plane at the gate.

![](https://i0.wp.com/www.joelonsoftware.com/wp-content/uploads/2007/10/13-02.jpg?w=730&ssl=1)

Where is it?

![](https://i0.wp.com/www.joelonsoftware.com/wp-content/uploads/2007/10/13-03.jpg?w=730&ssl=1)

Aha, looks like we’re waiting for flight 1726. Where’s that? I’ll check [FlightAware](http://www.flightaware.com):

![](https://i0.wp.com/www.joelonsoftware.com/wp-content/uploads/2007/10/13-04.jpg?resize=400%2C223&ssl=1)

Arriving 3:21. A bit later than the display at the gate is showing, and a total of 39 minutes late. And indeed, we took off almost exactly 39 minutes late.

Thanks to [Joe Farrell](http://www.elliott.org/blog/joes-holiday-air-travel-tips-its-us-vs-them/) for this idea.
