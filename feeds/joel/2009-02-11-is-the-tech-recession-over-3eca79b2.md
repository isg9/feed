# Is the tech recession over?

OK, it’s just one data point. All I know is sales of FogBugz and Copilot. But what I’m seeing is this: October-December 2008 were terrible—sales were 20% lower than usual—but starting January 5th, we saw a significant bounce *back* to the same level of sales as we had before this recession started, and it’s continued to this day.

[![](https://i0.wp.com/www.joelonsoftware.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/02/11PolarBear-thumbnail.jpg?w=730&ssl=1)](https://i0.wp.com/www.joelonsoftware.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/02/11PolarBear.jpg?ssl=1)This could be a fluke; it might not reflect any reality. Or it could be a sign that tech firms, for the moment, are doing reasonably well. The [Joel on Software job board](http://jobs.joelonsoftware.com/) is holding steady at about 50 jobs listed, down from a peak around 100, but there are still a significant number of openings for great developers.

Does this jive with your experience? (Comment over at the [Business of Software](http://discuss.joelonsoftware.com/default.asp?biz.5.733909.0)).
