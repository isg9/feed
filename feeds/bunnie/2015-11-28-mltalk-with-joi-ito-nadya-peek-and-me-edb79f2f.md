# MLTalk with Joi Ito, Nadya Peek and me

I gave an MLTalk at the MIT Media Lab this week, where I disclose a bit more about the genesis of the [Orchard platform](http://www.kosagi.com/w/index.php?title=Orchard_Main_Page) used to build, among other things, the [Burning Man sexually generated light pattern badge](http://www.bunniestudios.com/blog/?p=4484) I wrote about a couple months back.

The short provocation is followed up by a conversation with Joi Ito, the Director of the Media Lab, and [Nadya Peek](http://infosyncratic.nl/), a renowned expert in digital fabrication from [the CBA](http://cba.mit.edu/) (and incidentally, the namesake of the [Peek Array](http://spectrum.ieee.org/img/081LaptopChassisNoScreenBuildupTeardownScottTorborgCrowdSupplyFM7A2306-Edit-1445885708498.jpg) in the [Novena laptop](http://spectrum.ieee.org/consumer-electronics/portable-devices/novena-a-laptop-with-no-secrets)) about supply chains, digital fabrication, trustability, and things we’d like to see in the future of low volume manufacturing.

I figured I’d throw a link here on the blog to break the monotony of name that wares. Sorry for the lack of new posts, but I’ve been working on a couple of books and magazine articles in the past months (some of which have made it to print: [IEEE Spectrum](http://spectrum.ieee.org/consumer-electronics/portable-devices/novena-a-laptop-with-no-secrets), [Wired](http://www.wired.co.uk/magazine/archive/2015/08/features/moores-law)) which have consumed most of my capacity for creative writing.
