# On Liberating My Smartwatch From Cloud Services

I’ve often said that if we convince ourselves that [technology is magic, we risk becoming hostages to it](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aFrTJPfM58s&feature=youtu.be). Just recently, I had a brush with this fate, but happily, I was saved by open source.

At the time of writing, Garmin is [suffering from a massive ransomware attack](https://www.zdnet.com/article/garmin-services-and-production-go-down-after-ransomware-attack/). I also happen to be a user of the [Garmin Instinct](https://amzn.to/3hEi7YJ) watch. I’m very happy with it, and in many ways, it’s magical how much capability is packed into such a tiny package.

I also happen to have a hobby of paddling the outrigger canoe:

> Paddled around the island of Hong Kong in an outrigger canoe “change race” for the 2018 [#RHKYCATIR](https://twitter.com/hashtag/RHKYCATIR?src=hash&ref_src=twsrc%5Etfw) event! Stunning coastline and an unbeatable harbor view. Impressed at how [@RHKYC](https://twitter.com/RHKYC?ref_src=twsrc%5Etfw) pulled it off even after the typhoon devastated a lot of their equipment.
