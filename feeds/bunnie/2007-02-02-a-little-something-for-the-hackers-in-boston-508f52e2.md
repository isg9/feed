# A Little Something for the Hackers in Boston

I spent 10 good years in Boston while I attended MIT, and I carried around plenty of devices that could be classified as “suspicious looking” by Coakley’s standards, such as the [“nerd kits”](http://www.bunniestudios.com/bunnie/images/kit.gif) that [we used](http://web.mit.edu/spaik/www/pictures/cud.jpg) to build [our electronics labs](http://ocw.mit.edu/OcwWeb/Electrical-Engineering-and-Computer-Science/6-111Spring2004/CourseHome/index.htm).

So, for the hackers, students, and artists in Boston, here’s a little something for you. Give the Boston authorities a clue, because it seems like they need one:

[![](http://bunniestudios.com/blog/images/improvised.jpg)](http://www.cafepress.com/bunniestudios.105347482)

[![](http://bunniestudios.com/blog/images/dontpanic_rect_sm.jpg)](http://www.cafepress.com/bunniestudios.105349621)

These stickers are now prizes that you can choose if you win Name That Ware. If you are interested in getting one for yourself and not playing the game, you can buy these stickers by clicking on the respective images above \[cafepress.com\]. Let’s hope no terrorists buys one of these and sticks them on a *real* bomb, and hide them in plain sight–complete with blinking lights, loose wires, batteries, and the bird!

Only you can cure the American [Autoimmune Disease](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Autoimmunity)!
