# Open Source Geiger Counter Update

Today, March 11, marks the second anniversary of the Tohoku-Oki earthquake that devastated Japan and triggered the meltdown of Fukushima Daiichi. In a desire to help any way I could, I joined [Safecast](http://safecast.org) and created an [open source Geiger counter reference design](http://www.bunniestudios.com/blog/?p=2218), which I released last year around this time.

Since then, Safecast launched a successful [Kickstarter campaign](http://www.kickstarter.com/projects/seanbonner/safecast-x-kickstarter-geiger-counter), and [International Medcom](http://medcom.com/radiation-monitors/onyx-radiation-detector/) has completed the monumental tasks of tooling, testing, debugging, and [developing firmware](https://github.com/Safecast/onyxfirmware) for this little device.

Finally, the clear, limited-edition Kickstarter devices are shipping. You can see some photos of them on the [Make Blog](http://blog.makezine.com/2013/03/10/remembering-japan-contribute-to-a-fantastic-open-source-project/) as Eric Weinhoffer at Make is helping to put the finishing touch on every unit: a laser-cut, limited edition serial number on the back of each case.

Having shipped many products myself, I know how hard everyone has worked to make this a reality. Congrats guys!
