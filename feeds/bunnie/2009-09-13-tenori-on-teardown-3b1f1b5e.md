# Tenori-On Teardown

[Joi Ito](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joi_Ito) brought a neat toy to FOO Camp this year — a [Tenori-On](http://www.global.yamaha.com/design/tenori-on/). The Tenori-On is this very interactive, beautiful piece of hardware created by Toshio Iwai that, to paraphrase his words, creates a “visible music” interface with a shape and sound that fits the player organically, a sort of re-invention of the violin for the digital age. Here’s a neat [YouTube demo](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_SGwDhKTrwU) of the Tenori-On. I had seen this instrument for the first time in a fabulous live performance by [Cornelius](http://www.myspace.com/corneliusofficial) in Tokyo, and had since lusted for it.

Joi was an amazingly good sport, and let me do what I instinctively want to do when I see a piece of sexy hardware like this — undress it! Thankfully, I always travel with screwdrivers and a camera to capture rare opportunities like this, so I was able to capitalize on the moment. I did get it apart (and put back together again!!), and here are some photos of the innards to share with you. I’ll eventually throw these photos up on [ifixit](http://www.ifixit.com/Teardown), it’s a better site for sharing teardowns.

The brains of the Tenori-On. Nice piece of work, well laid out (very nice symmetry around all the parts, good isolation of analog and digital), some premium component choices.

[![](http://bunniestudios.com/blog/images/tenori_4_sm.jpg)](http://bunniestudios.com/blog/images/tenori_4.jpg)

Backside of the 256-switch + LED array. There’s a diode at each intersection of the keyboard scan array.

[![](http://bunniestudios.com/blog/images/tenori_5_sm.jpg)](http://bunniestudios.com/blog/images/tenori_5.jpg)

Front side of the 256-switch + LED array that’s behind the active button panel.

[![](http://bunniestudios.com/blog/images/tenori_6_sm.jpg)](http://bunniestudios.com/blog/images/tenori_6.jpg)

The backside of the front bezel, showing the array of buttons. The case is made out of cast Aluminum, which is a pretty expensive technology for a relatively low-run product. Each button is carefully designed to allow for a uniform spreading of the LED light while also allowing for easy pressing of the offset switch.

Thanks again to Joi for letting me open up this baby!
