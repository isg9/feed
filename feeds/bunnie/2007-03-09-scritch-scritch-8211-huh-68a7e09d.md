# Scritch scritch&#8211;huh?

I was poking at the [CIA World Fact Book](https://www.cia.gov/cia/publications/factbook/), which is a fascinating resource in itself, and noticed [this table of “current account balance” by country](https://www.cia.gov/cia/publications/factbook/rankorder/2187rank.html). It’s a big table, so I won’t quote it here, but to understand my puzzlement, take a look at the top ten names, and then look at the bottom ten names…and then notice the magnitude of each balance. One of these kids is not like the other…

Is this something to be concerned about? [Warren Buffet seems to think so](http://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/growing.pdf).

For contrast, try out this [table of GDP by country](https://www.cia.gov/cia/publications/factbook/rankorder/2001rank.html). This is closer to what I expected for the top 10 and bottom 10.

On a possibly related note, I was trying to import some Korean DRAM today, and it was stopped in customs, where I discovered that the US is charging a 40%(!) tariff on DRAM chips imported from certain Korean manufacturers. I guess this partially explains why the Idaho-based Micron seems to dominate the inventory in distributors in the US, and how they can charge a 2-3x price markup over what I can get overseas. Then again, it’s somewhat comforting to know that some of my DRAM comes from the same state that my french fries and potato chips do.

I guess the question is, would customers of finished products like the [chumby](http://www.chumby.com) pay extra just because it contains US-made DRAM.

I’m guessing not.
