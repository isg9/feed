# Creative Commons founders' fireside chat (Creative Commons blog)

Dee Harris has published a [summary](https://creativecommons.org/2026/06/30/inside-the-cc-founders-fireside-chat/) of the recent "fireside chat" featuring Creative Commons founders Hal Abelson, Lawrence (Larry) Lessig, Molly Van Houweling, and Glenn Otis Brown. The chat was to mark the [25th anniversary
of Creative Commons](https://creativecommons.org/25th-anniversary/) and included a look back at its history as well as a look at the landscape today:

> Twenty-five years ago, a small group of people made a bet. They believed that if you gave creators a simple set of tools and licenses in language that a lawyer, a machine, and a human could all read, millions of people might choose to share their work with the world instead of locking it down.

The [video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OOHUhSEdkB0) of the chat is available on YouTube.
