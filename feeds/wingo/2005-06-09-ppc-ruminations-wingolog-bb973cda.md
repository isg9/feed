9 June 2005 6:42 PM

Brian Mastenbrook [ruminates on Apple's shift to Intel](http://www.iscblog.info/blog/display/84) (via [planet lisp](http://planet.lisp.org/)). Rather interesting. My initial reaction was that this was bad for computer science to target one architecture, but Brian sees freedom in Apple's JIT compiler (supplied by [Transitive](http://www.transitive.com/)).

I occasionally find myself wondering what [hardware deficiencies Alan Kay is ranting about](http://acmqueue.com/modules.php?name=Content&pa=printer_friendly&pid=273&page=1) \-\- *Neither Intel nor Motorola nor any other chip company understands the first thing about why \[the Burroughs B5000\] architecture was a good idea*, etc. Wha?
