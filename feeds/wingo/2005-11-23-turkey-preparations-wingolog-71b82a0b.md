A few quick notes before my battery dies.

**turkey day**

For me Thanksgiving will fall on this Saturday. I'm used to it at this point -- it will be my fourth consecutive turkey day out of the states. The market in the center of town has turkeys, and I have a fellow there looking out for a 7-kilo specimen. We'll see what he finds tomorrow. Hopefully he doesn't decide to [run it through the meat cutter](//wingolog.org/archives/2004/11/30/turkles).

**[alice and bob](//wingolog.org/archives/2005/11/22/alice-and-bob) notes**

[John Borwick](http://johnborwick.com/) (sysadmin extraordinaire as well as friend from high school) wrote in to tell me that the [network time protocol uses the same strategy](http://www.ntp.org/ntpfaq/NTP-s-algo.htm#AEN1824) as the one I settled on. Of course I read a lot of papers before setting down to do simulations, but I never found a decent explanation of NTP. The FAQ entry he linked to summarizes it nicely. Thanks John!

So yes [Dave](http://blogs.gnome.org/view/bolsh/2005/11/23/0), it is a (somewhat embarrassing) real life problem -- ask any user of Flumotion that wants to synchronize capture from a webcam and a sound card. Also the rates of the clocks in question can vary (for example with temperature), and have a certain amount of jitter. Finally we're solving that issue now.

**random**

I signed up for catalan clases, finally. It's only been 10 months. How the time passes!
