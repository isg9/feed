Our time aligns on strange axes, sometimes. Last palindrome day, 11/11/11, found me walking the streets of Gothenburg with Werner Koch, the GPG maintainer. Werner said that in Germany, the carnival season opens on the 11th of November, at 11:11:11 in the morning. Today, 21022012, closes the carnival week here in Catalunya.

I was in Gothenburg for [FSCONS](http://fscons.org/). It so happens that the videos for the talk I gave there, [Guile: Free Software Means of Production](//wingolog.org/archives/2011/11/28/fscons-2011-free-software-free-society), just came out last week. So, as another point along that carnival axis, I offer in  form:

[Click to watch](http://videos.fscons.org/fscons/videos/FSCONS2011/guile-free-software-means-of-production/guile-free-software-means-of-production.webm)

Alternately you can [download the video](http://videos.fscons.org/fscons/videos/FSCONS2011/guile-free-software-means-of-production/guile-free-software-means-of-production.webm) directly (~112MB, 50 minutes). There are [notes](//wingolog.org/pub/fscons-2011-notes.pdf) too, a superset of the [slides](//wingolog.org/pub/fscons-2011-slides.pdf) from the talk.

As I said back then, this one was aimed at folks that didn't necessarily know very much about [Guile](http://gnu.org/s/guile/). It was also different from other talks in that it emphasized Guile as a general programming environment, not as an extension language. Guile is both things, and as the general-purpose side gets a lot less publicity, I wanted to emphasize it in this talk.

In the last 20 minutes or so, we did a live-hack. Inspired by a [tweet by mattmight](http://twitter.com/#!/mattmight/status/129231818984660992), we built [Bitter](http://areyoubitteraboutit.com/), a one-bit Twitter. I tried to convey what it's like to hack in Guile, with some success I think. Source code for the live-hack, such as it is, is linked to at the end of the page.
