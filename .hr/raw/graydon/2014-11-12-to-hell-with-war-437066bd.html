Today was Remembrance Day in Canada again. The CBC, the Governments of Canada and most of the corporate media in Canada spent a bunch of energy covering it. They did what they always do, more and more often with each passing year: glorify, valorize, promote, and honor war-making. And I am, again, absolutely disgusted by this behavior. It's monstrous.

I have written about this before, and I won't rehash exactly what I said there, though I still feel it. I just want to remind readers to stop and think, when they hear those positive words -- "hero", "sacrifice", "pride", "bravery", "honor", "service", "defend", "freedom" -- to reflect critically on what war really is, and what wars your country has fought in, and overall how leaders bring their people to war.

Don't let someone talk you into thinking WWII was acceptable just because it had a single easily-identified super-villain. He was manufactured in the trenches of WWI anyways. Moreover, think critically about the other ones. The ones you're being spoon-fed justification for. Wars are horror. Wars are atrocity. They are rape and slaughter. They are cluster bombs and land mines and firebombs and depleted uranium and napalm and mustard gas and artillery. Endless endless artillery. Blood, mud, metal, flames and horror. They are the ruin of people. They are limbs torn off. Faces burnt and mangled. Cities reduced to rubble. Hospitals and schools on fire. Healthy and happy bodies, minds, lives destroyed.



Anyone who tries to justify this sort of thing is doing you, and all humanity, a terrible crime.
